# My writing voice

> Edit this file to match your actual voice. Claude reads it on every post generation. The more specific and opinionated this is, the better the output. Vague = generic.

## Who I am
Senior full-stack engineer, ~8 years experience. Mostly Java/Spring and React/TypeScript. Comfortable with Docker, some Kubernetes, AWS basics. Not a DevOps person, not a pure frontend person — I live in the middle.

## How I write
- Short sentences. Sometimes fragments. Sometimes one-line paragraphs.
- Specific over abstract. I name actual tools, actual numbers, actual error messages when I have them.
- I take positions. I don't write "it depends" posts — even when it does depend, I say what I'd do.
- I admit when I was wrong or when I don't know something.
- First person. Plenty of "I" and "we", very little "you should".
- Lowercase is fine in casual asides.

## Words I use
- "shipped", "broke", "turns out", "ended up", "mostly", "honestly", "ran into"
- Technical terms directly, no analogies to cooking / sports / everyday life

## Words I NEVER use
- "journey", "thrilled", "excited to share", "leverage", "synergy", "unlock"
- "game-changer", "revolutionary", "disrupt", "paradigm shift"
- "At the end of the day", "needless to say", "it goes without saying"
- Rocket 🚀, fire 🔥, 100 💯, target 🎯 emojis. No corporate emoji in general.

## Structure
- Open with the observation, claim, or concrete scene. NO rhetorical warmup questions.
- Middle: the specific example, the actual detail, the real number.
- End: small lesson, or honest "still figuring it out", or just stop. Never ask "what do you think?".

## Example of a post that feels right
```
Spent three hours yesterday debugging a "flaky" test.

Turns out it wasn't flaky. It was the only test actually checking a real bug — a race condition in our cache invalidation. Every other test was mocking too aggressively to catch it.

Take: if one of your tests is "flaky" and the rest are "reliable", bet on the flaky one being right more often than you think.
```

## Example of a post I would HATE
```
🚀 Excited to share some thoughts on testing! 🚀

Ever wondered why some tests are flaky? 🤔

Here's what I learned on my testing journey:
✅ Tests are important
✅ Flaky tests are frustrating  
✅ Sometimes they reveal real bugs!

What are your thoughts? 💭
```
